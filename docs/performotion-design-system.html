Photography & Image Style
✓ Use
  • Real people, real bodies — not models
  • Clinical settings and gym floor together
  • 1:1 EP session moments (genuine)
  • Group Classes — inclusive, not performative
  • Recovery room (sauna, cold plunge)
  • Strength training — barbells, not machines
  • Warm natural light where possible
  • Navy/teal colour grading
✗ Avoid
  • Stock photos of perfect gym bodies
  • Overly filtered or stylised imagery
  • Bootcamp energy — people screaming
  • Medical/hospital sterile imagery
  • Before/after body transformation shots
  • Generic commercial gym equipment
  • Bright neon gym colour schemes
  • Overly corporate wellness feel
Landing Page Structure (Standard)
1. Hero — Headline + subheadline + primary CTA + trust signal (e.g. "EP-led • Strength-focused • Results-driven")
2. Problem / Positioning — Acknowledge the pain/struggle. Position vs the alternative (physio, generic gym, doing nothing)
3. Solution / Services — EP, Group Classes, Base, Recovery. Card layout. Each with a clear JTBD hook.
4. Social Proof — Client story or quote. Real, specific, identity-focused ("I stopped thinking of myself as injured")
5. The Pathway — Assess → Stabilise → Build → Maintain → Progress. Visual step flow.
6. Trust / Credentials — EP qualifications, facility, years in business, NDIS registered, etc.
7. CTA Section — Final CTA. Low friction. "Book a free consult" or "Talk to our team". No hard sell.
8. Footer — Nav, contact, location, social links
